Romanian Constitution (1991, revised 2003) - Key articles for Parliament understanding.

## Parliament Organization (Articles 58-71)

### Article 58 - Parliament Structure
- Parliament is the representative body of the Romanian people
- Consists of Camera Deputatilor (Chamber of Deputies) and Senatul (Senate)
- Elected for 4-year terms

### Article 63 - Legislature Duration  
- 4 years from date of election
- New elections within same day for both chambers

### Article 64 - Parliamentary Sittings
- Sittings are public
- Quorum required for debates/voting
- Decisions by majority of present members

### Article 65 - Electoral Legislation
- Parliament passes electoral legislation
- Must be approved by 2/3 of members

### Article 67 - Equality of Rights
- All MPs equal before law
- No special privileges based on elections

### Article 68 - Incompatibility
- Cannot be Deputy AND Senator simultaneously
- Cannot hold another public office

### Article 69 - Inviolability
- MPs cannot be prosecuted for opinions/votes
- Cannot be searched/detained without Chamber/Senate approval

### Article 70 - Eligibility
- Chamber: minimum 23 years
- Senate: minimum 33 years
- Must be Romanian citizen

### Article 71 - Mandate Freedom
- Mandate is imperative (represents nation, not just voters)
- Cannot be revoked by voters

## Legislative Process (Articles 72-78)

### Article 72 - Legislative Scope
- Laws establish rules for society
- Must respect constitutional principles

### Article 73 - Types of Laws
- Constitutional Revision (2/3 majority)
- Organic Laws (majority of all members)  
- Ordinary Laws (majority of present)

### Article 74 - Legislative Initiative
- Government
- Deputies/Senators
- Citizens (100,000+ signatures, except for certain areas)

### Article 75 - First Chamber
- Chamber: treaties, organic laws on specific areas
- Senate: all other matters for first review
- 45/60 day time limit for decisions

### Article 76 - Voting Procedures
- By law type: constitutional, organic, ordinary
- Different majorities required

### Article 77 - Promulgation  
- President must promulgate within 20 days
- Can send back for reconsideration (once)

### Article 78 - Effective Date
- Law enters force on publication date or specified future date

## Censorship Motion (Articles 113-114)

### Article 113 - Confidence Motion
- Parliament can call confidence vote on Government program/policy
- Requires majority of all members

### Article 114 - Censorship Motion
- Requires 1/4 of all members (minimum 116)
- Debated after 3 days
- Requires majority of all members (233) to pass
- If passed, Government must resign

## Key Points for Tracking

1. **Age requirements**: Chamber 23+, Senate 33+
2. **Law types**: constitutional/organic/ordinary (different thresholds)
3. **Initiative types**: Government/proposal/citizen
4. **First chamber**: Determines which chamber debates first
5. **Promulgation**: Presidential approval required
6. **Monitorul Oficial**: Laws enter force upon publication
